Pricing Analysis

Two layers: entry / participation fees (what the tournament charges to enter) and full traveler package price (all-in per player). Headline finding: every major European benchmark publishes pricing openly — our four events publish essentially none.

The transparency gap: Gothia, Norway Cup, Dana Cup and Donosti all post per-team and per-person prices on their own sites. Our events (Surf Cup Int'l, Royal Pre-Season, IDA Easter Cup) are quote-only / bundled — the only public numbers come from third-party operators. That's both a credibility gap and a competitive-intel gap.

What the numbers say

  • We're the only ones hiding price. Benchmarks publish; we quote-only. Price-shoppers and coaches comparing options can't find us in the consideration set.
  • Super Cup NI ~$1,200/player is land-only — not comparable to the ~$2,000–2,300 airfare-inclusive benchmarks. Like-for-like it's mid-market.
  • Our positioning justifies a premium. "Play vs. Barça/Real/PSG academies" + hotel/college lodging points to the upper-mid/premium band (~$2,500–$4,000+ pp) — make sure we're capturing it, not underpricing.

Pricing moves to consider

  • Publish "from" pricing (even a range) — the giants do; it builds trust and captures search/comparison demand.
  • Offer tiered packages (school vs. 3-star vs. 4-star) like Donosti/Gothia — lets families self-select and raises the ceiling.
  • Separate land vs. air in quotes so buyers compare apples-to-apples and see the value.
  • Get the internal quote sheets into this model to benchmark our real per-player economics vs. the field.

Strategic Takeaways

Where our tournament portfolio stands, and how to build it.

The one-line positioning

  • We are not a mass-participation festival — and shouldn't try to be. Our lane is the curated, all-inclusive, elite travel-and-play experience in premium destinations, for pro-academy and ambitious youth squads. Peer set: Dallas Cup, Generation adidas Cup, MIC elite tier.

Where we win / can press

  • Experience > scale. Full travel package, premium venues (Salou, Rome, Reading, Valencia), pro-club opponents — a product the Nordic mega-cups don't sell.
  • A calendar pathway. Easter (IDA/Valencia) → July (Super Cup NI) → August (Royal pre-season) → autumn (Surf Cup Int'l). Package it as one elite journey and cross-sell across events.
  • Surf brand halo + GSE travel machine + IDA development pipeline = an integrated funnel no standalone tournament has.
  • Under-served niche: Gen adidas Cup's elite model, but delivered as a commercial experience to visiting (US) teams.

Risks / gaps to close

  • Prestige is asserted, not established for the newer events (Surf Cup Int'l, Royal). They lean on the Surf brand halo and marketing numbers.
  • Borrowed IP: Super Cup NI is our most prestigious event but we don't own it — we're a travel/delivery partner. Value can be renegotiated away.
  • IDA Easter Cup is brand-new, small, and regional (mostly Spanish clubs) — not yet a benchmark.
  • Unverified stats undercut credibility with sophisticated buyers; the giants publish audited numbers.

Recommended next actions

  • Build owned-IP prestige. Invest behind Surf Cup International and IDA Easter Cup as flagship owned events — track and publish verified team/nation/club data to earn credibility.
  • Package the "elite tour pathway" as a marketed product (multi-event journey across the calendar) — the differentiator vs. one-off tournaments.
  • Deepen or hedge Super Cup NI. Lock in a longer/exclusive delivery agreement, or develop an owned summer equivalent so prestige isn't rented.
  • Target the Dallas / Gen adidas segment — position against them on experience + destinations, not scale.
  • Use the events as GSE tour anchors — every tournament entry is a full travel package sale; measure revenue per participating team, not team count.
  • Refresh quarterly — verify figures and watch MIC/Donosti/IberCup moves into the US and elite space.
